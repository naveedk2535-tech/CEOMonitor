WTI vs Brent — Daily Price (90 Days) ?
NYMEX WTI futures (gold) vs ICE Brent futures (blue dashed) from Yahoo Finance. When Brent pulls away from WTI, global supply is tighter. When they converge, markets are balanced.
US Crude Inventory (Weekly, 52 Weeks) ?
EIA weekly US commercial crude oil stocks (WCRSTUS1). Green bars = inventory drew down (bullish — tighter supply). Red bars = inventory built up (bearish — oversupply). Sustained draws push prices higher.
US Gasoline Demand (Weekly) ?
EIA weekly US gasoline product supplied (WGFUPUS2) — a proxy for real consumer demand. Rising demand = more crude consumed = bullish for oil prices. Seasonal peaks in summer driving season.
Brent-WTI Spread ($/bbl) ?
Brent price minus WTI price. Normal range is $3-$6. Widening spread (>$6) = global supply tighter than US = bullish for WTI to catch up. Narrowing (<$1) = global weakness or US oversupply.
